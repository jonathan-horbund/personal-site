## Ingredients

- 2 lbs ripe Roma tomatoes, halved
- 1 head of garlic, top cut off to expose cloves
- 1 medium yellow onion, quartered
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- ½ tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes
- 2 cups vegetable broth
- ¼ cup heavy cream (optional)
- Fresh basil, for serving
- Good crusty bread, for serving

## Steps

### 1. Roast the vegetables

Preheat your oven to 400°F. On a large sheet pan, arrange the tomato halves cut-side up, along with the onion quarters. Nestle the garlic head among them. Drizzle everything generously with olive oil, season with salt, pepper, and paprika.

Roast for 45 minutes, until the tomatoes are collapsed and slightly caramelized at the edges, and the onion is soft and golden. The garlic should be tender enough to squeeze out of the skin.

### 2. Build the soup

Transfer the roasted vegetables to a large pot. Squeeze the roasted garlic cloves out of their skins directly into the pot. Add the crushed tomatoes and vegetable broth. 

Bring to a simmer over medium heat and cook for 10 minutes, letting everything meld.

### 3. Blend

Use an immersion blender to blend until smooth, or work in batches with a stand blender. If using a stand blender, let the soup cool slightly first, and don't fill the blender more than halfway.

Taste for seasoning. Add the cream if you want something richer and more velvety.

### 4. Serve

Ladle into bowls. Tear fresh basil over the top. Have bread ready. Eat while it's hot.

## Notes

- The soup freezes well — make a double batch and freeze half in individual portions.
- For a spicier version, add a pinch of red pepper flakes with the paprika.
- You can skip the cream entirely; the soup is excellent without it.
