## Ingredients

- 2¼ cups all-purpose flour
- 1 tsp baking soda
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 2 sticks (1 cup) unsalted butter
- ¾ cup granulated sugar
- ¾ cup packed brown sugar
- 2 large eggs
- 2 tsp vanilla extract
- 2 cups chocolate chips (a mix of semi-sweet and bittersweet is ideal)
- Flaky sea salt, for finishing

## Steps

### 1. Brown the butter

In a light-colored saucepan over medium heat, melt the butter, stirring occasionally. Continue cooking until it foams, then turns golden, then smells nutty and appears amber with brown bits at the bottom — about 5–7 minutes. 

Pour into a large mixing bowl and let cool for 15 minutes. This step is non-negotiable.

### 2. Make the dough

Whisk both sugars into the cooled brown butter until combined. Add eggs one at a time, whisking well after each. Add vanilla.

Fold in the flour, baking soda, and salt until just combined. Fold in the chocolate chips.

Refrigerate the dough for at least 1 hour (overnight is better).

### 3. Bake

Preheat oven to 375°F. Scoop dough into balls (about 2 tablespoons each) and place 2 inches apart on a parchment-lined baking sheet.

Bake 10–12 minutes, until the edges are set and golden but the centers still look slightly underdone. They'll set up as they cool.

Immediately sprinkle with flaky sea salt.

### 4. Wait (the hardest step)

Let them cool on the pan for 5 minutes before transferring. If you eat one immediately you'll burn your mouth and also the chocolate chips are structurally unstable. Wait.

## Notes

- Chilling the dough is what gives you the chewy interior. Don't skip it.
- These keep for 3 days in an airtight container. They don't last that long.
- You can freeze unbaked dough balls and bake straight from frozen — add 2–3 minutes to bake time.
