## Problem

Cardholders had access to transaction history but no easy way to understand their spending patterns. Engagement with the financial tools section of the app was low. The ask: build a spend insights feature.

## Approach

Before writing a single requirement, we spent three weeks in discovery. We interviewed 20 cardholders about how they currently track spending, what questions they ask themselves about money, and what tools they already use.

The key finding: people weren't asking "how much did I spend?" They were asking "why is my balance lower than I expected?" and "am I spending more on food than usual?"

That shift — from historical reporting to proactive insight — changed everything.

## Process

We scoped an MVP around three "insight types":

1. **Anomaly detection** — "Your restaurant spending is 2x your average this month"
2. **Category summaries** — Simple monthly breakdowns in plain language
3. **Recurring charge visibility** — Surface subscriptions and recurring billings clearly

We worked closely with data engineering to define the signals, and with design to find a visual language that felt informative without feeling surveillance-y.
