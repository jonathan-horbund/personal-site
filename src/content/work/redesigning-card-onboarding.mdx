## Problem

Credit card applicants were dropping off at an unusually high rate during identity verification — a step that should feel like a formality but instead felt like a wall.

The business felt the impact in lost revenue. Customers felt it as frustration. Our job was to understand why, and fix it without compromising compliance requirements.

## Approach

We started by pulling data. Where exactly were people leaving? How long were they spending on each step? Were there patterns by device, channel, or customer segment?

Then we talked to people. 12 usability sessions, a mix of completed and abandoned applications. We discovered three distinct problems:

1. **Language mismatch** — legal copy was written for compliance, not humans
2. **Progress invisibility** — applicants didn't know how many steps remained
3. **Recovery friction** — errors during document upload were opaque and hard to fix

## Process

We ran two rounds of prototype testing, iterating on each finding:

- Rewrote verification step copy in plain language, reviewed by compliance
- Added a visible progress bar with step labels
- Redesigned error states to include specific guidance and retry affordances

Each prototype was tested against the prior version before shipping.
